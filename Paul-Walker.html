His Biography and His death

Paul Walker was born in the fall of 1973 in the family of a former boxer and model. His family since childhood
supported Paul's mother's desire to introduce the baby into the world of show business. When Walker was one and
a half years old, he had already managed to star in a diaper commercial.From an early age, Paul was a regular at the
castings to which his mother had taken him. The boy went to the catwalk and starred for clothing brands, then he got roles
in his first series. Parents were proud that their son appeared on the screen and on the catwalks, but Paul himself did not
particularly like the modeling business. At that time, he was very attracted to marine biology. He watched documentaries by
Jacques Yves Cousteau and dreamed of one day going to study the underwater world. Paul went on to graduate as a marine
biologist and was involved in developing projects and filming documentaries for National Geographic. As a child, Paul
independently made aquariums for fish. To some extent, Paul chose to act out of a sincere desire to prove to his
brothers that he was capable of succeeding in this area. Almost all the men in his family certainly became
representatives of traditional male professions: policemen, workers, construction. Paul's brothers often made fun of
their older brother, who had chosen a more sophisticated role, but Walker was in no hurry to 'give up.Even despite
the uncertainty and complexes that had settled his family's doubts in him, he decided to succeed in his chosen career,
and one day his dream came true.
In his youth, Paul was a light guy, passionate about surfing and fishing. He received a nickname from his friends,
which they think perfectly characterized the actor at that time: they all called him "The Tramp "However, that did
not stop Walker from taking his work seriously. movie theater. The 90s became a springboard for him, which helped
the future celebrity to rise to fame. He starred in Young and Restless and then in the comedy film Meet the Didles,
which brought him great popularity. One of the most memorable projects that Paul was working on at the time, Walker
himself considers the film Pleasantville, on the set of which he managed to work with Tobey Maguire and Reese Witherspoon.
This film was followed by unsuccessful auditions for the Star. The Wars franchise and Paul's loudest, most popular
and brilliant project, which brought him not only triumphant popularity, but also decent fees - "Fast and Furious".
In the summer of 2001, the first picture appeared on the screens, on which neither the directors nor the performers
at first had any special hopes. Paul got the role of a positive character - a policeman with high moral qualities,
for which the audience loved him so much. On the screen, a friendship was born between Brian and Dominic, whom
Diesel embodied, and this friendship turned into a real affection that accompanied the actors for many years.
Vin and Paul have occasionally admitted in interviews that they treat each other like brothers. Diesel even named
his daughter after a friend - Paulina. A few years later, Paul starred in a sequel to the film, after which
he became the main character when Vin Diesel went to conquer fantasy films. Acting finally began to please Walker.
He often said he liked action movies - that way he felt more masculine and confident. However, Paul didn't stop
at action films: she also dabbled in a war movie and continued to study marine biology which fascinated him. Walker
started a charity that supports disaster victims. Paul not only sponsored the foundation, he went on expeditions
alone to help people. One of the actor's passionate hobbies was motorsport. He constantly participated in rallies
and races, being a good driver, however, ironically, it was this passion that killed Walker. In November 2013, he
drove home with his friend, who was driving a two-seater and at one point lost control. The passenger and driver
were killed instantly when they hit a concrete pole.Walker has a daughter, Meadow Rain, with ex-girlfriend Rebecca
McBrain. In his will, Walker left his $25 million estate to Meadow and requested that his mother become her legal
guardian.

His Legacy - ROWW ( Reach Out WorldWide)

Reach Out WorldWide (ROWW) is a 501(c)(3) registered nonprofit founded by Paul Walker. In January of 2010, after
a massive earthquake devastated Haiti, Paul spontaneously organized a relief team that responded to the disaster.
On the trip, Paul saw a gap between the availability of skilled resources and the requirement for such personnel in
post-disaster situations. Upon returning from Haiti, ROWW was established with the purpose of fulfilling this unmet
need. ROWW continues to respond internationally to natural disasters worldwide ranging from earthquakes to
hurricanes. Our teams are small so that we can gather and deploy quickly. We pack efficiently so that we can carry
our gear into the most remote and desperate regions. These are the people that have been excluded from the media
attention and are too small or inconvenient for the larger non-profits to reach. It would have been far easier and
cheaper for Paul to put his face on an existing organization and check in from time to time, but he didn't. He chose
to get dropped off from a helicopter in Haiti, during a very unstable time, without a plan in hopes to
personally help those in need. He accomplished his goal and was hooked. Next, he shifted his schedule around to go
to aid those in need in Chile. Again he not only funded the trip but gave his own time. After a few more deployments
he was finally at peace with his profession as he knew it was allowing ROWW to exist.
